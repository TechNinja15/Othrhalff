# Game Ideas That Fit OthrHalff

## Air Hockey Date Duel

A fast 1v1 air hockey game for matched users. Each match can have themed tables like campus canteen, fest night, library after-hours, or hostel common room.
(It varies what's best and what suites us)


Why it fits:
- Easy to understand instantly.
- Works well on mobile.
- Competitive without feeling too serious.
- Great first game for realtime 1v1 multiplayer.

Connection twist:
- Winner unlocks a playful chat prompt.
- After the match, both players get a "chemistry moment" recap.
- Rematch button can say "best of three?"

## Space Sparx

Two players control small ships in a colorful space arena. They collect hearts, dodge asteroids, and shoot obstacles. The goal is less about destroying each other and more about surviving chaos together.

Why it fits:
- Feels arcade-like and energetic.
- Fits the Sparx brand naturally.
- Can be made cute, flirty, and visually memorable.

Connection twist:
- Shared combo boosts when both players collect matching items.
- "Orbit mode" where players get bonus points for staying close.
- End screen shows "sync score" based on movement and choices.

## Campus Pong

A simple 1v1 pong-style game with campus-themed arenas. Tables can be named after campus zones like Lecture Hall, Canteen, Parking Lot, Fest Stage, or Library.

Why it fits:
- Classic and lightweight.
- Very low learning curve.
- Easy to make fast and polished.

Connection twist:
- Powerups are based on dating app moments: Seen, Ghosted, Crush, Confession, Spark.
- Winning a rally can reveal a tiny icebreaker question.

## Heart Rush

Both players race around a small arena collecting symbols. Matching symbols give bonus points. Wrong symbols cause short slowdowns.

Why it fits:
- More connection-focused than combat-focused.
- Easy to make campus-romantic without being cringe.
- Good for quick 30-60 second sessions.

Connection twist:
- If both players choose the same symbol at the same time, they get a "sync bonus."
- Final result shows shared vibe: chaotic, soft, bold, mysterious, or golden retriever energy.

## Confession Dodge

Anonymous confession bubbles fall from the top. Players dodge awkward confessions and collect cute, funny, or relatable ones.

Why it fits:
- Directly connects to the existing Confessions feature.
- Has meme potential.
- Can reuse confession-style UI language.

Connection twist:
- Collected confessions become prompts after the game.
- Players can vote "relatable" or "too real" at the end.

## Cupid Clash

A tiny 1v1 projectile arena where players shoot heart arrows, dodge charm blasts, and use silly powerups.

Why it fits:
- Feels playful and brand-aligned.
- More action-heavy than air hockey, but still light.
- Good second or third multiplayer game after the room system is stable.

Connection twist:
- Damage can be called "heartbreak."
- Shields can be called "boundaries."
- Winner gets a "Cupid called it" badge for the session.

## Library Whisper

A stealth mini-game where both players try to pass notes across a library without getting caught by moving "faculty vision cones."

Why it fits:
- Very campus-specific.
- Funny, charming, and different from generic arcade games.
- Can be cooperative or 1v1.

Connection twist:
- Notes contain small icebreaker prompts.
- If both players successfully pass notes, they unlock a shared question.

## Fest Frenzy

A split-screen or shared-arena game where players collect fest passes, dodge crowd waves, and race to the main stage.

Why it fits:
- Campus fests are emotionally strong social moments.
- Gives the app a local student identity.
- Can support seasonal events.

Connection twist:
- Players pick their ideal fest role: performer, photographer, foodie, organizer, wanderer.
- Results can recommend a virtual date activity.

## Chai Run

Players race through a campus map to deliver chai before it gets cold. Obstacles include speed bumps, late buses, crowded corridors, and random attendance checks.

Why it fits:
- Very relatable for Indian campus culture.
- Light, funny, and casual.
- Can be single-player, co-op, or 1v1.

Connection twist:
- Each round ends with a "chai or coffee?" prompt.
- Score screen shows "date energy" based on speed and choices.

## Ghost Mode Tag

One player is visible, the other becomes semi-invisible and tries to tag or avoid them. Roles switch every round.

Why it fits:
- Plays with the app's anonymous/ghost identity theme.
- Simple but unique.
- Good for short sessions.

Connection twist:
- Visibility increases when players get closer.
- End screen can say "you found each other" when both players score evenly.

## Match Maze

Players start on opposite sides of a maze and race to meet in the middle while collecting clues about each other.

Why it fits:
- Symbolically matches the app's core idea.
- More wholesome and connection-driven.
- Can work as a casual first-date mini-game.

Connection twist:
- Clues are profile interests, choices, or anonymous hints.
- If both players reach the center, they unlock a shared conversation card.

## Red Flag Runner

A humorous runner game where players dodge red flags and collect green flags. It should stay playful and avoid harsh or toxic framing.

Why it fits:
- Very social-media-friendly.
- Easy to understand.
- Works well as a short mobile game.

Connection twist:
- At the end, players get a funny "green flag score."
- Can generate a lighthearted chat prompt like "what is your most underrated green flag?"

## Vibe Volleyball

A 1v1 volleyball-style game where the ball changes based on the vibe: chill, chaos, romantic, academic, party, or mystery.

Why it fits:
- More expressive than basic pong.
- Campus sports angle.
- Fun with simple physics.

Connection twist:
- Players vote for the next vibe between rounds.
- Shared choices create bonus effects.

## First Date Dash

Players race through tiny date scenarios and make quick choices: where to sit, what snack to pick, which song to play, how to respond.

Why it fits:
- It is directly tied to dating and connection.
- Great for converting gameplay into conversation.
- Can be funny without becoming too serious.

Connection twist:
- After the game, show compatibility based on decisions.
- Give both users one unlocked "ask them this" prompt.

## Roommate Radar

A campus chaos game where players identify who belongs to which room, club, or department based on funny clues.

Why it fits:
- Campus culture specific.
- Good for local events and community play.
- Could become a broader campus party game.

Connection twist:
- Matched users can compare guesses.
- Group mode can be added later for campus events.

## Best First MVP

Start with Air Hockey Date Duel.

Reasons:
- Cleanest 1v1 multiplayer loop.
- Smallest shared state.
- Mobile friendly.
- Easy to polish.
- Easy to theme around OthrHalff.
- Reusable multiplayer foundation for later games.

Recommended tech:
- KAPLAY for the 2D game client.
- Colyseus for realtime 1v1 game rooms.
- Supabase for auth, invites, profiles, game results, campus leaderboard, and rewards.

Next games after Air Hockey:
- Space Sparx.
- Campus Pong.
- Heart Rush.
- Confession Dodge.
